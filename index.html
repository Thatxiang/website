I am an Assistant Professor of Finance at the University of Connecticut School of Business. My research lies at the intersection of corporate finance, financial technology, and innovation, with a focus on bankruptcy and financial distress, venture capital, and the economics of technological change.

My work has appeared in the Journal of Finance, the Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis, and the Journal of Law and Economics. It has been featured in Carroll School News and cited in congressional testimony before the House Judiciary Subcommittee. I have also been featured in the NBER Reporter and ProMarket.

Before joining UConn, I completed my PhD in Finance at Boston College.

Publications

Published & Accepted

How Can Innovation Screening Be Improved? A Machine Learning Analysis with Economic Consequences for Firm Performance

Journal of Financial and Quantitative Analysis, Vol. 60, No. 8, December 2025, pp. 3722–3752.

FMA Best Paper Award in FinTech, 2021  ·  Kuldeep Shastri Outstanding Doctoral Student Paper, 2021

[ssrn]
This paper studies how machine learning can improve innovation screening and examines the economic consequences for firm performance. We develop a new measure of innovation quality using textual analysis and machine learning, and show that it predicts future firm performance beyond existing measures.

Can Small Businesses Survive Chapter 11?

with Edith Hotchkiss (Boston College) and Benjamin Iverson (BYU)

Journal of Finance, forthcoming.

Featured in Carroll School News  ·  Cited in House Judiciary Subcommittee Hearing

[ssrn]
This paper examines whether small businesses can successfully reorganize under Chapter 11 of the U.S. Bankruptcy Code. We find that small businesses face distinct challenges in bankruptcy but that targeted reforms can improve their reorganization outcomes.

How Does Private Firm Patenting Affect Anti-Takeover Provisions in Corporate Charters? Evidence from Initial Public Offerings

with Thomas Chemmanur (Boston College), Manish Gupta (Nottingham), and Karen Simonyan (Suffolk)

Journal of Law and Economics, forthcoming.

[ssrn]
We examine the relationship between private firm patenting activity and the adoption of anti-takeover provisions in IPO charters. Using patent data prior to firms' IPOs, we show that greater patenting activity leads to stronger takeover defenses at the time of going public.

Working Papers

From Competitors to Partners: Banks' Venture Investments in Fintech

with Manju Puri (Duke) and Yiming Qian (UConn)

R&R Journal of Financial Economics

Featured in NBER Reporter

[ssrn]
We study banks' corporate venture capital investments in fintech startups. Using novel data on bank-fintech investment relationships, we show that banks strategically invest in fintech companies that complement rather than compete with their core businesses.

GSE Restrictions, Credit Supply, and Rental Market Spillovers

with Natee Amornsiripanitch (Philadelphia Fed), Philip Strahan (Boston College), and Song Zhang (Delaware)

R&R Management Science

[ssrn]
We examine how government-sponsored enterprise (GSE) lending restrictions affect credit supply in mortgage markets and generate spillover effects on rental housing markets.

Do Fintech Shadow Banks Compete with Technological Advantages? Evidence from Mortgage Lending

with Sijie Wang (CUHK Shenzhen) and Siyi Shen

R&R Management Science

[ssrn]
We study whether fintech shadow banks compete with traditional lenders through superior technology. Using mortgage lending data, we identify the technological advantages of fintech lenders and their competitive effects on bank lending.

How Does VC Engagement Direct Startup Experimentation?

with Xuelin Li (Columbia), Sijie Wang (CUHK Shenzhen), and Jiajie Xu (Iowa)

Featured in ProMarket

[ssrn]
We examine how venture capital investor engagement shapes the direction of startup experimentation and product development.

Woke or Broke? The Impact of Corporate Activism on Product Sales

with Meng Gao (UConn)

[ssrn]
We study how corporate social and political activism affects product sales, exploiting variation in the nature and timing of firm activism events.

What is the Role of the Options Market? Evidence from Newly Public Companies

with Thomas Chemmanur (Boston College) and Chayawat Ornthanalai (Toronto)

[ssrn]
We examine the role of options markets for newly public firms, using IPO data to study how options listing affects price discovery and firm value.

The Economic Impact of Insolvency Reforms Around the World

with James Albertus (CMU), Edith Hotchkiss (Boston College), and Aurelio Gurrea-Martinez (SMU)

Draft available soon

We study the economic effects of insolvency law reforms across countries, examining how changes in bankruptcy regimes affect firm investment, credit access, and economic outcomes.

Visual Cues and Valuation: Evidence from the Housing Market

with Puja Bhattacharya (Arkansas), Sherry Li (Arkansas), Yu Wang (Arkansas), and Cedric Wu

AEA RCT Registry: AEARCTR-0016398

[ssrn]
We conduct a randomized controlled trial to study how visual cues affect home valuations, using professional photography and staging treatments in a real estate market context.
